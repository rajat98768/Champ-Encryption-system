Low Level

Caesar, ROT13, Atbash, Vigenère, Polybius — classical paper-and-pencil ciphers. Educational value; easily broken by modern cryptanalysis.

Mid Level

Rail Fence, Columnar Transposition, Playfair, ADFGVX — used in WWI/WWII-era military communications. More resistant to frequency attacks.

High Level

XOR stream cipher, simplified Feistel, RC4-style PRNG — modern algorithmic primitives. Computationally harder to break without the key.

Ultra — CHAMP

Vigenère → Polybius → XOR layered stack. Applies confusion (Vigenère), diffusion (Polybius), and stream randomisation (XOR). Product cipher per Shannon.

Index of Coincidence

English IC ≈ 0.0667. Random IC ≈ 0.0385. The Frequency Analysis tab measures how well the cipher masks language patterns.

Shannon's Theory

Confusion obscures the key-ciphertext relationship. Diffusion spreads plaintext statistics. CHAMP applies both through layering.
